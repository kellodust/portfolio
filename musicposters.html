Poster Design

String Chamber Ensemble

This poster, like most of the posters on this page, are a result of my time spent designing for BYU-Idaho's Department of Music. This poster was one of my last that I designed for the department. With every poster designed, there is also a TV ad, social media images, calendar images, web ads and occasionally banners.

Piano Majors Recital

I've always thought posters are an ideal canvas for implementing several design elements and principles in the same design. My motivation with every poster is to do that as much as possible without taking away from the information on the poster. Instead, I want to use typographic heirarchy, for example, to my advantage in displaying this type of information. This poster is an example of how the heirarchy and negative space worked together to make it cohesive.

Derek Brown Poster

Derek Brown is a saxophone beat-boxer. I wanted his poster to appear as fun and unique as he is. My research for this project was watching videos of his past performances and learning how to portray music through design. I think the "research" paid off because he later posted the image on all his social media platforms, mentioned how much he loved it in the concert, and gave me a signed copy of his album.

Jazz Ensemble

The description I received of the concert contained the words "patriotic jazz". After sketches and illustrations, I came up with something I felt portrayed the description accurately.

universityOrchestra

This project also gave me a unique project description. I was told that the music played would combine time travel and world travel. I didn't want the design to be overwhelming so I felt that using different skylines from different times and places would be a good way to communicate the theme. I made sure that the places and the times were the same ones that would be represented in the concert.

Chamber Winds

This poster project focused on the principle of negative space. I find that the more I focus on incorporating the principles of design into my work, the happier I am with it.
